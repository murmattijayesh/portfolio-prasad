## Overview

Quiet electric and hybrid vehicles are difficult for pedestrians to hear at low
speeds, creating a real safety risk. This final-year thesis develops a
regulatory-compliant Acoustic Vehicle Alerting System (AVAS) that emits a
context-aware audible warning to actively reduce that risk. The project is
ongoing.

## Highlights

- Researching and applying advanced psychoacoustics and signal processing to design an effective, context-aware warning signal
- Targeting compliance with AVAS regulatory standards for quiet-vehicle operation
- Authored and submitted a review paper on AVAS for pedestrian safety to the *Traffic Injury Prevention Journal* (Q1)
